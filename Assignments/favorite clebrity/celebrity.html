Nle choppa

Bryson Lashun Potts (born November 1, 2002), known professionally as NLE Choppa (previously YNR Choppa), is an American rapper from Memphis, Tennessee. He rose to fame with his 2019 single "Shotta Flow", which was certified platinum by the RIAA and peaked in the top-40 of the Billboard Hot 100. The song was included on his debut EP titled Cottonwood, released the same year. NLE Choppa released his debut studio album, Top Shotta, in August 2020. It peaked in the top ten of the US Billboard 200 and included the singles "Camelot" and "Walk Em Down" (featuring Roddy Ricch), both of which peaked in the top 40 of the Billboard Hot 100. His third and fourth mixtapes, From Dark to Light (2020) and Me vs. Me (2022), preceded his second studio album, Cottonwood 2 (2023), which also contained his highest-charting single, "Slut Me Out".
